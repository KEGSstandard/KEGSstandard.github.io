Andy Burnham to "transform Britain"

Andy Burnham has set out his blueprint to transform the UK with a promise to improve living standards and restore faith in politics through the “biggest rebalancing of power our country has ever seen”.

The person widely expected to be the next prime minister said the current system was “broken” and that “more of the same” would not be enough to tackle the significant challenges faced by the country.

In a wide-ranging speech anchored by an ambition to “lift the country back up” and to revive hope among fed-up voters, Burnham said there needed to be a dramatic “change in how Britain is governed, not just who governs it”.

With Burnham now all but certain to take over from Keir Starmer in less than three weeks’ time, his address was intended to show what direction he will take the country, and reassure both Labour MPs and voters who may feel he is untested, and even unprepared, for power.

In his speech, Burnham once again sought to reassure that he would stick to Rachel Reeves’s fiscal rules, after he suggested last year the UK was “in hock” to bond markets, saying his programme was backed by the “discipline that comes from sound public finances”.

Burnham, who served in the last Labour government and sat on the opposition benches until he became mayor of Greater Manchester, said his generation of politicians had to take responsibility for not being good enough.

England In The World Cup

Tuchel’s England Reach the round of 16

World Cup- Thomas Tuchel has officially taken charge of England’s national team, and the Three Lions now have a completely different playstyle. After speculation and questions about whether the former Chelsea and Bayern Munich manager could adapt to international football, Tuchel has delivered a clear message: England will play on the front foot.

Tuchel’s arrival has brought a level of intensity and structure England fans are excited about. Training sessions have been described as “relentless,” with players pushed to operate in tight spaces, recycle possession quickly, and press with coordinated aggression. Several squad members have privately admitted that Tuchel’s tactical detail is “unlike anything they’ve experienced at international level.”

The biggest shift has been England’s shape. Tuchel has implemented a flexible 3‑4‑3 system, allowing England to dominate transitions while giving creative freedom to attacking stars like Jude Bellingham and Phil Foden. Harry Kane has praised Tuchel’s tactics, saying the team “finally feels like it has a clear plan in every phase of play.”

Whether this new‑look England can win the world cup to be seen, but one thing is clear: under Thomas Tuchel, England are a clear contender for the World Cup title.

England's group stage matches have also proved that Tuchel is a manager worthy to get your hopes up for. As showun in the opening group stage game against Croatia (4-2 win) England can play with a wonderful attack and good defense. After a frustrating 0-0 draw against the heavily defensive Ghana, Tuchel has proved himself again after a masterclass 2-1 win against Panama. Hopes for a world cup title are at an all-time high.
